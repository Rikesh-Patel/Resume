Mission Statement

Francis Bacon once said, "Knowledge is power." Now, centuries later, data is power. To reach this stage, I apply myself. From the first moments with my computer, I have always had a fondness for number games. These games with rows and rows of digits itched the quantitative side of my brain, and to this day I could still enumerate the classics. Of course, all the numbers in the game were relevant. For analysis, the more the merrier. Essentially to win the game, I needed to make the numbers larger, so I did. Everything from Google sheets to Excel, from putting a weight on my mouse to Python-scripting and macros; if it made me more money, saved time, or got an achievement in the game, I would do it. The peak of this fascination was clicking ‘start’ and then lying back in my chair as some authority took hold of my screen in a predetermined manner.

Without knowing it, this past time would lead me up the path of data modeling. This is my grand scheme-to generate these efficiencies, lionize strengths, snip weaknesses for the most elaborate game of them all. To me, data is a whole new world where potential is limitless. Imagine the possibilities. I love creating, editing videos, writing story lines, and designing graphics, so with my foundation in econometrics and working experience with data visualization, I yearn to make great strides.

I live for it.
